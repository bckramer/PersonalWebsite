Cumulative Reflection

ISU has prepared me for a lot of things, and I consider my time here to have been incredibly valuable. This report will cover these aspects in detail, and include a summary of many different aspects of being an engineer at Iowa State.

At ISU, I have also become more effective in groups thanks to in-depth group courses like SE 491 and COM S 309. I've also had plenty of classes that incorporated valuable group project into the curriculum. However, I have had little exposure to classes that highlight contemporary issues in engineering. This would have been valuable in helping me see where my skills could be applied. That being said, I do feel I am equipped with an understanding of professional and ethical duties

When doing projects in my ISU courses, I used a variety of resources. Perhaps most importantly was StackOverflow. That website provides tons of answers from the most simple to the most complex. I have also learned a ton from working with other students, and in return, have helped impart what I've learned onto other students.

As an executive member of the Game Development Club, I have become a much more effective and comfortable presenter, organizer, and leader. Interacting with underclassmen and giving them the resources they need to be successful in something they care about has been a tremendously rewarding experience. This in turn has made me a better group partner and more organized student.

To keep myself sharp and further explore personal interests, I have undertaken game development as my primary hobby. In the process, I have become a better programmer, while picking up non-engineering related skills like visual art and music production. This has made me more comfortable with taking on new, unknown problems.

If I were an undergrad, I believe my life would be a lot more organized than it was as a freshman! I also believe I would work in a more effective way and come up with not only a correct solution, but a sophisticated one as well. Growing as an engineer to me means learning how to make things, and make them the right way. If I were an undergrad again, I would slow down and come up with a solution that solves the problem and makes the most sense.

Ethics Essay

I chose to write about the case study involving Theranos. If I was a developer of this company, I would like to believe I would have left before it became known that the product was 'vaporware.' More so, I would like to believe I would have tried to expose the lies of the company. However, the latter action is tricky. The case study makes it seem as if whistleblowers were hit with harsh legal action, which helps squash any attempts to show the company for what it really is.

I believe the engineers did what they did because attempts to expose the company would have likely entailed legal action, and furthermore, if the engineers ever did figure out how to make the product Holmes claims she could the wave of fame and fortune would have been immense. This creates string negative and positive incentive to stick with the company and ride it out as long as you can.

About Me

Hello! My name is Benjamin Kramer and I am a software engineering student and developer at Iowa State University. My passion for technology began about the time I first played Star Fox on the Nintendo 64. From then on, I became fascinated with how games worked, and eventually how software as a whole worked. Once I dug my teeth into coding in high school, I realized how expansive and powerful software was. From then on, it was clear I had to pursue a career in the field.

To get a head start on this pursuit, I started playing around with C# and the Unity game engine in high school. While the best I could come up with at the time was a text adventure game and a 3D marble rolling game, it gave me a great foundation for coding. Basic concepts were easier to relearn when I took my first coding class my senior year of high school. Here I picked up skills in Python. The assignments we were given were my first experiences with difficult coding challenges. By the time I was in college, all my basic skills were solidified, giving me a chance to sharpen them even further in my introductory courses.

By the spring of my freshman year, I was fortunate enough to be hired as a student developer at Iowa State. Over the summer, this meant forty hour work weeks of constant challenges, which made for great advancement in my coding ability.

More recently, my passion led me to my first hackathon, hosted at Iowa State University. Here I developed my first neural network with a team of two other students. Since then, I've found myself thinking about artificial intelligence and its uses more frequently. In the future, I'd love to work on more AI focused projects.

Personal Projects

Myrtl, the Genetic AI

My favorite personal project is one a team of two students and I did for the 2017 Fall Hackathon at Iowa State. We built a simple game, a genetic algorithm and a neural network in Java in about thirty hours named Myrtl. The program would play the game, at first trying random actions, and then through genetic mutations and neural network modifications, learn how to play the game. It was my first foray into the world of AI and one of the most challenging projects I've ever done. The team and I learned a ton about neural networks, machine learning and AI.

I was in charge of the construction of the neural network, which was built by scratch in Java. No libraries were use, which meant I had to have a concrete grasp of the network and what requirements it had to have if I was going to be successful. There were many times in which it was very difficult to conceptualize and visualize the network, but through perseverance I came out successful. I also lent my hand in debugging and tweaking the genetic algorithm, which take several network and selects the best traits to be passed onto a new generation.

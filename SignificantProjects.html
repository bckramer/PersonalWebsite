Pocket Aces, the Program that Plays Poker

Pocket Aces was me and a friend's final project for a graduate course at ISU: HCI 575: Computational Perception. Pocket Aces utilized computer vision to analyze a computer poker program, identifying cards, pot sizes, public cards and more. After it had analyzed the screen, it would make a decision after feeding the information into a neural network. The project was done entirely in Python using OpenCV and TensorFlow.

My main task was implementing the computer vision aspect of Pocket Aces, and I'm happy to report that Pocket Aces was highly accurate at identifying the desired information. The approach was to use structuring elements to identify areas of interest on the screen, but this method was not always perfect. One of my proudest achievements on the project, was developing my own algorithm that, given an image, will identify areas of interest on its own and then dissect the information in the image. Myrtl, the Genetic AI

Myrtle is a project a team of two students and I did for the 2017 Fall Hackathon at Iowa State. We built a simple game, a genetic algorithm and a neural network in Java in about thirty hours named Myrtl. The program would play the game, at first trying random actions, and then through genetic mutations and neural network modifications, learn how to play the game. It was my first foray into the world of AI and one of the most challenging projects I've ever done. The team and I learned a ton about neural networks, machine learning and AI.

I was in charge of the construction of the neural network, which was built by scratch in Java. No libraries were use, which meant I had to have a concrete grasp of the network and what requirements it had to have if I was going to be successful. There were many times in which it was very difficult to conceptualize and visualize the network, but through perseverance I came out successful. I also lent my hand in debugging and tweaking the genetic algorithm, which takes several network and selects the best traits to be passed onto a new generation.
